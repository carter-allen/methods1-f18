  1. 1
  2. 0
  3. 10
  4. 5
  5. None of the above

Let \(X \sim N(0,10)\) and \(Y \sim N(5,2)\), independently. What is \(Var[X-Y]\)?

  1. 10
  2. 0
  3. 5
  4. 12
  5. None of the above
  • \(Var[X-Y] = Var[X] + Var[Y] - 2Cov[X,Y] = 10 + 2 - 0 = 12\)

Why do we even need to distinguish between probability and confidence?

  • We are estimating parameters, which are fixed quantities. It does not make sense to make probability statements about the parameter. We instead make statements about our confidence bounds, which are considered random variables.
